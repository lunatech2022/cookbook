Hidden Veggie Mac & Cheese

⏱️ 45 min 📂 Dinner 🍽️ 6 servings
~450
Calories
20g
Protein
5g
Fiber
55g
Carbs

Ingredients

  • 2 cups butternut squash, peeled and cubed (½-inch)
  • 1½ cups cauliflower florets
  • 1 cup carrots, peeled and sliced (¼-inch thick)
  • 4 cups water (for boiling vegetables)
  • 16 oz elbow macaroni (or cavatappi, shells)
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk (whole milk for creaminess)
  • 1 cup sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
  • ½ cup Gruyère or fontina cheese, shredded (or more cheddar)
  • ½ cup cream cheese, cubed
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard (optional — enhances cheese flavor)
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • ¼ tsp paprika (for color)
  • ½ cup breadcrumbs or crushed crackers
  • 2 tbsp melted butter
  • ¼ cup shredded cheddar

Instructions

  1. **Peel and cube squash:** Use sharp vegetable peeler on butternut squash. Cut off ends, halve lengthwise, scoop out seeds with spoon. Cut into ½-inch cubes. Measure 2 cups.
  2. **Prep carrots:** Peel, slice into ¼-inch rounds. Measure 1 cup.
  3. **Prep cauliflower:** Cut into small florets. Measure 1½ cups.
  4. **Boil water:** Fill large pot with 4 cups water. Bring to boil over **high heat** (level 8-9 of 10), about 5-7 minutes.
  5. **Cook vegetables:** Add squash, cauliflower, and carrots all at once. Reduce heat to **medium-high** (level 6). Cook 12-15 minutes until all vegetables are very soft — a fork should pass through with zero resistance. The carrots should be the firmest; test one.
  6. **Drain and set aside:** Drain in colander. Let cool 2 minutes. You're blending these hot, so some cooling helps prevent steam pressure in blender.
  7. **Boil pasta water:** In the same large pot, bring 4-5 quarts fresh water to rolling boil over **high heat**. Add 1 tbsp salt.
  8. **Cook macaroni:** Add pasta, stir immediately. Cook per package directions minus 2 minutes (usually 6-7 minutes for elbows). You want it **very al dente** — it will cook more in the oven. It should be edible but have noticeable firmness.
  9. **Reserve water:** Scoop out 1 cup pasta water before draining.
  10. **Drain:** Drain pasta in colander. Return to pot (off heat). Set aside.
  11. **Blend vegetables:** Add cooked vegetables to blender. Add ½ cup milk. Blend on **high** for 60-90 seconds until completely smooth — no flecks, no texture, just like a thick soup. Scrape down sides once.
  12. **Check:** Should be pourable but thick. If too thick (won't pour), add 2-3 tbsp more milk and blend 10 seconds.
  13. **Make roux:** In a large saucepan or Dutch oven, melt 4 tbsp butter over **medium heat** (level 5 of 10). When foam subsides (about 1 minute), whisk in ¼ cup flour. Cook 1-2 minutes, whisking constantly. The roux should look like pale yellow paste and smell slightly nutty — not brown.
  14. **Add milk gradually:** Pour in remaining 1½ cups milk in a slow stream, whisking constantly to prevent lumps. Whisk until smooth. The mixture will thicken in 2-3 minutes. You're looking for consistency of heavy cream.
  15. **Add blended vegetables:** Pour in the smooth vegetable puree. Whisk to combine. The sauce should now look orange — this is your "cheese sauce" base.
  16. **Add cheeses:** Reduce heat to **low** (level 2 of 10). Add cheddar, Gruyère, cream cheese, Dijon (if using), garlic powder, salt, pepper, and paprika. Stir constantly with wooden spoon until all cheese is melted — about 4-5 minutes.
  17. **Taste and adjust (critical step):**
  18. **Bland?** Add ¼ tsp salt
  19. **Not cheesy enough?** Add ½ cup more shredded cheddar
  20. **Too thick?** Stir in reserved pasta water 2 tbsp at a time
  21. **Too thin?** Simmer 2-3 more minutes on low
  22. **Want more "cheese" flavor?** Add 1 tsp more Dijon or 1 tbsp nutritional yeast
  23. **Mix:** Pour cheese sauce over cooked pasta. Stir until every piece is coated. Taste a piece — it should be creamy and flavorful.
  24. **Serve now (stovetop version):** Portion into bowls, top with extra cheese. Done.
  25. **OR bake (for crispy topping):** Preheat oven to **375°F (190°C)**. Transfer mac and cheese to a greased 9x13 inch baking dish. Mix breadcrumbs with melted butter, sprinkle on top, then add ¼ cup shredded cheddar. Bake 15-20 minutes until bubbly and top is golden brown. Let cool 5 minutes before serving.

🧒 Kid Adaptation

  • Don't mention the vegetables — just call it "mac and cheese"
  • If kids are suspicious of orange color, use less carrot and more cauliflower (lighter color)
  • Let them add their own toppings — breadcrumbs, extra cheese, even ketchup on the side
  • Cut into fun shapes with cookie cutters if serving to toddlers
  • Serve with a familiar side: sliced apples, grapes, or breadsticks

📦 Batch Cooking

  • **Refrigerate:** Up to 4 days in airtight container
  • **Reheat:** Add 2-3 tbsp milk per serving, microwave 90 seconds, stir well
  • **Freeze:** Baked version freezes excellently for up to 3 months. Wrap individual portions in foil, then place in freezer bag. Reheat from frozen: Bake covered at 350°F (175°C) for 25-30 minutes, then uncover 5 minutes.
  • **Make-ahead:** Prep sauce and pasta separately, refrigerate up to 2 days. Combine and bake when ready.
