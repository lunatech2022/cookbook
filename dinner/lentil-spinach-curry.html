Lentil & Spinach Curry

⏱️ 40 min 📂 Dinner 🍽️ 4 servings
~380
Calories
18g
Protein
14g
Fiber
52g
Carbs

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp coconut oil or olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced (about 1 cup)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated (or ½ tsp ground ginger)
  • 2 tbsp curry powder (mild or medium)
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • ¼ tsp cayenne (optional, for heat)
  • 1½ cups dried green or brown lentils (rinsed)
  • 4 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes, undrained
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk (full-fat for creaminess)
  • 4 cups fresh baby spinach (about 5 oz)
  • 1 tsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • ½ lime (for juice)
  • Cooked brown rice, quinoa, or naan bread
  • Fresh cilantro (optional)

Instructions

  1. **Dice onion:** Cut off both ends, peel, slice in half, then cut into small ¼-inch pieces.
  2. **Mince garlic:** Peel cloves. Chop finely or use a garlic press.
  3. **Grate ginger:** Peel fresh ginger with a spoon. Grate on the fine side of a box grater.
  4. **Rinse lentils:** Place in a strainer. Rinse under cold water until water runs clear. Remove any debris or stones.
  5. **Heat oil:** In a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat 2 tbsp oil over **medium heat** (level 5 of 10 on most stoves). Let it heat 1-2 minutes. Oil is ready when it shimmers and coats the bottom evenly — it should look thinner and move easily when you tilt the pot. If it smokes, it's too hot; remove from heat 30 seconds.
  6. **Sauté onion:** Add diced onion all at once. Stir to coat in oil. Cook **5-6 minutes**, stirring every 30-45 seconds. You're aiming for **soft and translucent** (edges look slightly see-through, color is pale yellow, not brown). If edges start browning before 5 minutes, reduce heat to medium-low. If after 6 minutes they're still crunchy, increase heat slightly. The onion should smell sweet, not sharp.
  7. **Add aromatics:** Add garlic and ginger. Stir constantly for **1 minute exactly**. You'll smell a strong fragrant aroma — that's your cue. If it starts smelling acrid or looking golden-brown quickly, reduce heat immediately. Burnt garlic = bitter curry.
  8. **Toast spices:** Add curry powder, cumin, turmeric, and cayenne. Stir constantly for **30-45 seconds**. The spices will form a thick paste with the oil and aromatics. You'll smell a warm, toasty, fragrant aroma (like toasted nuts). The mixture will look darker and slightly stick to the bottom — that's the fond (flavor). Do not walk away; spices burn in under 60 seconds.
  9. **Deglaze immediately:** Add ¼ cup of the broth and scrape the bottom with a wooden spoon or spatula. All the brown bits should release. This happens in 10-15 seconds.
  10. **Add lentils & remaining liquid:** Add rinsed lentils, remaining broth, and diced tomatoes (with all their juice). Stir well. The liquid should cover lentils by at least 1 inch. If not, add ½ cup water.
  11. **Bring to boil:** Increase heat to **high**. Bring to a full rolling boil — big bubbles breaking the surface across the whole pot. This takes **5-7 minutes** depending on your stove. Stir once halfway so lentils don't stick.
  12. **Reduce to simmer:** Once boiling, reduce heat to **low** (level 2-3 of 10). Cover with a tight-fitting lid. You want a gentle simmer — small bubbles just breaking the surface occasionally. If it's bubbling vigorously, reduce heat further. If no bubbles at all, increase slightly.
  13. **Simmer lentils:** Cook **20-25 minutes**, stirring every 7-8 minutes to prevent sticking. Set a timer. Check at 20 minutes:
  14. **Done:** Lentils are tender, can be easily mashed with a fork, but still hold their shape (not dissolved into mush)
  15. **Not done:** Still firm in center, give 3-5 more minutes
  16. **Too mushy:** You overcooked — still edible, just thicker
  17. **Liquid too low:** Add ¼ cup hot water or broth if lentils look dry before they're tender
  18. **Add coconut milk:** Remove lid. Stir in coconut milk thoroughly. Increase heat to **medium-low**. Simmer **uncovered** for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally. The curry should thicken slightly — it coats the back of a spoon. If too thin, simmer 2-3 more minutes. If too thick, add ¼ cup broth.
  19. **Add spinach:** Remove from heat. Stir in fresh spinach by handfuls, one at a time. Each handful wilts in 20-30 seconds. All spinach should be fully wilted and dark green within 2 minutes. Don't overcook or it turns slimy and dark.
  20. **Season:** Add 1 tsp salt. Squeeze in juice of ½ lime (about 1 tbsp). Stir. Taste. Adjust:
  21. **Bland?** Add more salt, ¼ tsp at a time
  22. **Flat?** More lime juice (acidity brightens everything)
  23. **Not "curry" enough?** Add ½ tsp more curry powder
  24. **Too spicy?** Stir in 2 tbsp more coconut milk
  25. **Rest:** Let curry sit off heat, uncovered, for **5 minutes**. It thickens as it cools slightly, and flavors meld. Stir before serving.
  26. Ladle over cooked rice or quinoa. Garnish with cilantro if desired.

🧒 Kid Adaptation

  • Use mild curry powder (no cayenne)
  • Serve deconstructed: plain lentils + plain rice + spinach on the side
  • Let them add their own coconut milk (they love the drizzle)
  • Mild version: reduce curry powder to 1 tbsp
  • Serve with naan bread for dipping

📦 Batch Cooking

  • Makes 4 generous servings — perfect for leftovers
  • Refrigerates up to 5 days
  • Freezes excellently for up to 3 months (without spinach — add fresh when reheating)
  • Reheat on stove over medium-low with splash of broth
  • Double the recipe and freeze half
