Lentil & Spinach Curry

⏱️ 40 min 📂 Dinner 🍽️ 4 servings
~380
Calories
18g
Protein
14g
Fiber
52g
Carbs

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp coconut oil or olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced (about 1 cup)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated (or ½ tsp ground ginger)
  • 2 tbsp curry powder (mild or medium)
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • ¼ tsp cayenne (optional, for heat)
  • 1½ cups dried green or brown lentils (rinsed)
  • 4 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes, undrained
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk (full-fat for creaminess)
  • 4 cups fresh baby spinach (about 5 oz)
  • 1 tsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • ½ lime (for juice)
  • Cooked brown rice, quinoa, or naan bread
  • Fresh cilantro (optional)

Instructions

  1. **Dice onion:** Cut off both ends, peel, slice in half, then cut into small ¼-inch pieces.
  2. **Mince garlic:** Peel cloves. Chop finely or use a garlic press.
  3. **Grate ginger:** Peel fresh ginger with a spoon. Grate on the fine side of a box grater.
  4. **Rinse lentils:** Place in a strainer. Rinse under cold water until water runs clear. Remove any debris or stones.
  5. **Heat oil:** In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat oil over medium heat (oil should shimmer, about 1-2 minutes).
  6. **Sauté onion:** Add diced onion. Cook 5-6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until soft and translucent (not browned).
  7. **Add aromatics:** Add garlic and ginger. Cook 1 minute until fragrant — don't let garlic burn or it turns bitter.
  8. **Toast spices:** Add curry powder, cumin, turmeric, and cayenne. Stir constantly for 30 seconds. Toasting spices unlocks their flavor.
  9. **Add lentils & liquid:** Add rinsed lentils, broth, and diced tomatoes (with juice). Stir to combine.
  10. **Simmer:** Bring to a boil over high heat (5-7 minutes). Then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until lentils are tender but not mushy.
  11. **Add coconut milk:** Stir in coconut milk. Simmer uncovered 5 more minutes to thicken slightly.
  12. **Add spinach:** Stir in fresh spinach by handfuls. It wilts in 1-2 minutes.
  13. **Season:** Add salt. Squeeze in lime juice. Taste and adjust — add more salt, lime, or curry powder as needed.
  14. **Rest:** Let curry sit off heat for 5 minutes. It thickens as it cools slightly.
  15. Ladle over cooked rice or quinoa. Garnish with cilantro if desired.
