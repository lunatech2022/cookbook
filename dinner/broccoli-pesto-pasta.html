Broccoli Pesto Pasta

⏱️ 25 min 📂 Dinner 🍽️ 4 servings
~380
Calories
16g
Protein
7g
Fiber
52g
Carbs

Ingredients

  • 3 cups broccoli florets (about 1 medium head)
  • 1 cup fresh spinach (packed)
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice (about ½ lemon)
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • ¼ cup pasta cooking water (reserved)
  • 12 oz pasta (bow ties, penne, or spirals work great)
  • 4 quarts water
  • 1 tbsp salt (for pasta water)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup cooked chicken breast, diced
  • 1 cup white beans, drained and rinsed
  • Extra Parmesan for serving

Instructions

  1. **Prep broccoli:** Cut broccoli into small florets (bite-sized pieces). Peel the thick stem and chop it into ½-inch pieces — it's edible and nutritious.
  2. **Prep spinach:** If using baby spinach, no chopping needed. If using mature spinach, remove tough stems.
  3. **Prep garlic:** Peel cloves. If raw garlic is too strong for your kids, you can use ½ tsp garlic powder instead, or roast the garlic first (wrap in foil, bake 400°F/200°C for 30 minutes until soft and sweet).
  4. **Juice lemon:** Roll lemon on counter under palm for 10 seconds. Cut in half, squeeze out 2 tbsp juice, remove seeds.
  5. **Boil water:** Fill large pot with 4 cups water. Bring to boil over **high heat** (level 8-9 of 10), about 5-7 minutes.
  6. **Blanch broccoli:** Add broccoli florets and stems. Cook **3-4 minutes** until bright green and fork-tender but not mushy. You want it soft enough to blend but not falling apart.
  7. **Add spinach:** In the last 30 seconds, add spinach on top. It wilts instantly.
  8. **Drain and shock (optional):** Drain in colander. For brighter green color, rinse briefly with cold water (stops cooking). This step is optional but recommended if you're not blending immediately.
  9. **Boil pasta water:** In the same pot, bring 4 quarts fresh water to rolling boil over **high heat**. Add 1 tbsp salt.
  10. **Cook pasta:** Add pasta, stir immediately. Cook per package minus 1 minute (usually 8-10 minutes). Test at 8 minutes — should be al dente with tiny white center when bitten.
  11. **Reserve water:** Before draining, scoop out 1 cup pasta water. The starch is liquid gold for sauce.
  12. **Drain:** Drain pasta, return to pot (off heat).
  13. **Blend:** Add cooked broccoli, spinach, Parmesan, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper to a blender or food processor.
  14. **Add pasta water:** Pour in ¼ cup reserved pasta water. This helps everything blend smoothly and creates emulsion.
  15. **Blend until smooth:** Process on **high** for 60-90 seconds. Scrape down sides once. The pesto should be:
  16. Bright green (not brown or olive)
  17. Completely smooth — no visible broccoli chunks
  18. Thick but pourable, like a smoothie
  19. **Check and adjust:**
  20. **Too thick?** Add 1-2 tbsp more pasta water, blend 10 seconds
  21. **Too thin?** Add 2 tbsp more Parmesan
  22. **Too garlicky?** Add 1 tbsp more olive oil and 1 tsp lemon juice
  23. **Needs salt?** Add ¼ tsp at a time
  24. **Toss:** Pour pesto over hot pasta. Stir thoroughly until every piece is coated. If it seems dry, add pasta water 1 tbsp at a time.
  25. **Add extras (optional):** Fold in cherry tomatoes, cooked chicken, or white beans now.
  26. **Serve:** Portion into bowls. Top with extra Parmesan. Serve immediately — pesto loses its bright green color as it sits.

🧒 Kid Adaptation

  • Call it "Green Monster Pasta" or "Hulk Pasta" — make it fun, not scary
  • If kids are skeptical of green food, start with ½ the spinach, gradually increase
  • Let them sprinkle their own cheese — involvement increases willingness
  • Serve cherry tomatoes on the side (not mixed in) if they're suspicious
  • For very picky eaters, mix pesto with a little regular marinara at first (half and half)

📦 Batch Cooking

  • **Pesto freezes excellently:** Make double batch, freeze in ice cube trays, transfer to bag. Each cube = 1 serving. Keeps 3 months.
  • **Refrigerate pesto:** Up to 5 days in airtight container. Top with thin layer of olive oil to prevent browning.
  • **Pasta:** Best fresh. Does not freeze well.
  • **Meal prep:** Make pesto Sunday, cook fresh pasta each night. Takes 10 minutes.
