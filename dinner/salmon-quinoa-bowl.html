Salmon Quinoa Bowl

⏱️ 35 min 📂 Dinner 🍽️ 2 servings
~550
Calories
38g
Protein
7g
Fiber
42g
Carbs

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dry quinoa (rinsed)
  • 2 cups water or low-sodium broth
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 2 salmon fillets (5-6 oz each, skin-on or skinless)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ lemon (for juice)
  • 2 cups broccoli florets (bite-sized)
  • 1 large sweet potato, cubed (½-inch pieces)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ tsp paprika
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 2 tbsp tahini or Greek yogurt
  • Optional: sesame seeds, fresh dill

Instructions

  1. **Rinse quinoa:** Place quinoa in a fine-mesh strainer. Rinse under cold water for 30 seconds. This removes bitterness.
  2. **Boil:** In a medium saucepan, combine rinsed quinoa, water/broth, and salt. Bring to a boil over high heat (3-4 minutes).
  3. **Simmer:** Reduce heat to low. Cover with a tight-fitting lid. Simmer 15 minutes. Do NOT lift the lid — steam cooks it.
  4. **Rest:** Remove from heat. Let stand covered 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork. Keep warm.
  5. **Preheat oven:** Set oven to 425°F (220°C). Position rack in center.
  6. **Prep veggies:** Toss broccoli and sweet potato cubes with 1 tbsp olive oil and paprika on a baking sheet. Spread in a single layer — don't overcrowd or they'll steam instead of roast.
  7. **Roast:** Bake 20-22 minutes until sweet potatoes are tender when pierced with a fork and edges are slightly browned. Stir halfway through (at 10 minutes).
  8. **Prep salmon:** Pat fillets completely dry with paper towels. Moisture prevents searing.
  9. **Season:** Brush both sides with 1 tbsp olive oil. Sprinkle with salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
  10. **Heat pan:** Place a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Let it heat 2-3 minutes until very hot.
  11. **Sear skin-side down:** Place salmon skin-side down (or presentation side down if skinless). Press gently with spatula for 10 seconds so it makes full contact. Cook 4-5 minutes without moving.
  12. **Flip:** Turn salmon over. Cook 2-3 more minutes for medium (internal temp 125°F / 52°C) or 4-5 minutes for well-done (145°F / 63°C). Salmon is done when it flakes easily with a fork and is opaque throughout.
  13. **Finish:** Squeeze lemon juice over salmon immediately after removing from heat.
  14. **Build bowls:** Divide quinoa between two bowls. Top with roasted broccoli, sweet potato, and salmon fillet.
  15. **Finish:** Add avocado slices. Drizzle with tahini or yogurt. Sprinkle with sesame seeds or dill.
