Salmon Quinoa Bowl

⏱️ 35 min 📂 Dinner 🍽️ 2 servings
~550
Calories
38g
Protein
7g
Fiber
42g
Carbs

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dry quinoa (rinsed)
  • 2 cups water or low-sodium broth
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 2 salmon fillets (5-6 oz each, skin-on or skinless)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ lemon (for juice)
  • 2 cups broccoli florets (bite-sized)
  • 1 large sweet potato, cubed (½-inch pieces)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ tsp paprika
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 2 tbsp tahini or Greek yogurt
  • Optional: sesame seeds, fresh dill

Instructions

  1. **Rinse quinoa:** Place quinoa in a fine-mesh strainer. Rinse under cold water for 30 seconds. This removes bitterness.
  2. **Boil:** In a medium saucepan, combine rinsed quinoa, water/broth, and salt. Bring to a boil over high heat (3-4 minutes).
  3. **Simmer:** Reduce heat to low. Cover with a tight-fitting lid. Simmer 15 minutes. Do NOT lift the lid — steam cooks it.
  4. **Rest:** Remove from heat. Let stand covered 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork. Keep warm.
  5. **Preheat oven:** Set oven to 425°F (220°C). Position rack in center.
  6. **Prep veggies:** Toss broccoli and sweet potato cubes with 1 tbsp olive oil and paprika on a baking sheet. Spread in a single layer — don't overcrowd or they'll steam instead of roast.
  7. **Roast:** Bake 20-22 minutes until sweet potatoes are tender when pierced with a fork and edges are slightly browned. Stir halfway through (at 10 minutes).
  8. **Prep salmon:** Remove from fridge 15 minutes before cooking (cold fish cooks unevenly). Pat fillets completely dry with paper towels — moisture is the enemy of a good sear. Check for pinbones by running your finger along the surface; remove any with tweezers.
  9. **Season:** Brush both sides with 1 tbsp olive oil. Sprinkle with salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Press seasoning gently into the flesh so it sticks.
  10. **Heat the pan:** Place a large heavy-bottomed skillet (cast iron, stainless steel, or non-stick) over medium-high heat. Let it heat 3-4 minutes. Test if ready: flick a few drops of water onto the pan — they should sizzle and evaporate in 2-3 seconds. If they just sit there, pan isn't hot enough. If they vanish instantly, reduce heat slightly.
  11. **Add oil:** Pour 1 tsp oil into the pan. It should shimmer immediately and move like water (not look thick or sticky). If it smokes, remove pan from heat for 30 seconds, then return.
  12. **Sear skin-side down:** Carefully place salmon skin-side down (or presentation side down if skinless). It should sizzle loudly — if it doesn't, pan wasn't hot enough. Press gently with spatula for 10 seconds so full surface makes contact. **Do not move it.** Set a timer:
  13. **Skin-on:** 4-5 minutes (skin gets crispy)
  14. **Skinless, thick fillet (1½+ inches):** 4 minutes
  15. **Skinless, thin fillet (under 1 inch):** 3 minutes
  16. **Check for release:** After 3-4 minutes, gently nudge with spatula. If it sticks, it's not ready — leave it. When properly seared, it releases easily.
  17. **Flip:** Use a thin spatula, slide under from the edge, and flip in one confident motion. The seared side should be deep golden-brown (not black). Cook 2-3 minutes on second side for medium, or 3-4 minutes for well-done.
  18. **Check doneness (pick one method):**
  19. **Thermometer (best):** Insert into thickest part — 125°F (52°C) = medium, 130°F = medium-well, 145°F (63°C) = well-done per FDA
  20. **Fork test:** Insert fork at thickest part and gently twist. Medium = slight resistance in center, flakes but still translucent in middle. Well-done = flakes easily, fully opaque, no translucency
  21. **Visual cue:** The fat (white protein) starts pooling on the surface = getting close to done
  22. **Rest:** Transfer salmon to a plate. Let rest 3 minutes — this allows juices to redistribute. Cover loosely with foil if your kitchen is cold.
  23. **Finish:** Squeeze fresh lemon juice over salmon immediately before serving.
  24. **Build bowls:** Divide quinoa between two bowls. Top with roasted broccoli, sweet potato, and salmon fillet.
  25. **Finish:** Add avocado slices. Drizzle with tahini or yogurt. Sprinkle with sesame seeds or dill.

🧒 Kid Adaptation

  • Serve components separately (deconstructed bowl)
  • Cut salmon into small flakes — easier to eat
  • Sweet potato is naturally kid-friendly
  • Skip tahini; use ketchup or ranch for dipping
  • Let kids build their own bowl

📦 Batch Cooking

  • Cook 2 cups dry quinoa (yields 6 cups cooked). Refrigerate up to 5 days
  • Roast double the veggies. Reheat in oven or air fryer
  • Salmon is best fresh, but can be refrigerated 2 days
  • Prep all components Sunday, assemble bowls fresh each night
