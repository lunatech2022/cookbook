Mini Veggie Pasta with Cheese Sauce

⏱️ 30 min 📂 Dinner 🍽️ 4 servings
~420
Calories
18g
Protein
6g
Fiber
58g
Carbs

Ingredients

  • 12 oz mini pasta shapes (shells, elbows, or mini penne)
  • 4 quarts water
  • 1 tbsp salt (for pasta water)
  • 2 cups butternut squash or sweet potato, peeled and cubed (½-inch pieces)
  • 1 cup cauliflower florets (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 cup milk (whole milk for creaminess)
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese (sharp or mild)
  • ¼ cup cream cheese
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ¼ tsp onion powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • Optional: ¼ tsp nutmeg (enhances cheese flavor)
  • Extra shredded cheese
  • Crushed whole-grain crackers or breadcrumbs
  • Chopped parsley

Instructions

  1. **Peel and cube squash:** Use a sharp vegetable peeler to remove butternut squash skin. Cut off top and bottom, slice in half lengthwise, scoop out seeds. Cut into ½-inch cubes. You need about 2 cups.
  2. **Prep cauliflower:** If using fresh, cut into small florets. If frozen, measure 1 cup — no need to thaw.
  3. **Boil water:** Fill a large pot with 4 cups water. Bring to a boil over **high heat** (level 8-9 of 10). This takes 5-7 minutes.
  4. **Cook veggies:** Add butternut squash and cauliflower to boiling water. Reduce heat to **medium-high** (level 6). Cook 10-12 minutes until very soft — a fork should slide through easily with no resistance.
  5. **Drain:** Reserve ½ cup cooking water, then drain vegetables in a colander. Set aside.
  6. **Boil pasta water:** In the same large pot, bring 4 quarts fresh water to a rolling boil over **high heat**. Add 1 tbsp salt — it should taste like seawater.
  7. **Cook pasta:** Add mini pasta. Stir immediately to prevent sticking. Cook according to package directions minus 1 minute (usually 7-9 minutes for mini shapes). You want it **al dente** — firm to the bite, not mushy.
  8. **Test at 7 minutes:** Fish out one piece and bite it. It should have a tiny white dot in the center (al dente) or be fully tender but still chewy. If crunchy, cook 1 more minute.
  9. **Reserve pasta water:** Before draining, scoop out 1 cup of the starchy pasta water. This helps thin the sauce later.
  10. **Drain pasta:** Drain in a colander. Do NOT rinse — the starch helps sauce cling. Return pasta to the empty pot (off heat).
  11. **Blend veggies:** Add cooked squash and cauliflower to a blender. Add milk, butter, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, pepper, and nutmeg if using. Blend on **high** for 60-90 seconds until completely smooth — no lumps, no visible vegetable pieces. Scrape down sides once if needed.
  12. **Check consistency:** The sauce should be thick but pourable — like heavy cream. If too thick, add 2-3 tbsp reserved pasta water and blend 10 more seconds. If too thin, don't worry — cheese will thicken it.
  13. **Add cheeses:** Pour sauce into a medium saucepan over **low heat** (level 2 of 10). Add shredded cheddar and cream cheese. Stir constantly with a wooden spoon or silicone spatula until cheese is fully melted — about 3-4 minutes. Do NOT boil — high heat makes cheese grainy and separates the fat.
  14. **Taste and adjust:**
  15. **Bland?** Add ¼ tsp more salt
  16. **Not cheesy enough?** Add 2 tbsp more shredded cheddar
  17. **Too thick?** Stir in 2-3 tbsp pasta water
  18. **Too thin?** Simmer 2 more minutes on low, stirring
  19. **Toss pasta:** Pour cheese sauce over drained pasta in the pot. Stir gently until every piece is coated. If sauce seems too thick, add pasta water 1 tbsp at a time until creamy.
  20. **Serve immediately:** Portion into bowls. Top with extra cheese, crushed crackers, or parsley if desired.

🧒 Kid Adaptation

  • Call it "cheesy pasta" or "macaroni cheese" — no need to mention vegetables
  • For very picky eaters, start with half the vegetable amount and gradually increase
  • Let kids sprinkle their own cheese on top (they eat what they make)
  • Serve with a side of something familiar (breadsticks, sliced apples)
  • Cut any larger pasta shapes smaller for toddlers

📦 Batch Cooking

  • **Refrigerate:** Store in airtight container up to 4 days
  • **Reheat:** Add 2-3 tbsp milk, microwave 60-90 seconds, stir well
  • **Freeze:** Sauce freezes well separately for 3 months. Pasta does NOT freeze well — gets mushy
  • **Prep ahead:** Make sauce on Sunday, refrigerate up to 5 days. Cook fresh pasta and reheat sauce on serving day
