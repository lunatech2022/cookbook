Berry Chia Oatmeal

⏱️ 10 min 📂 Breakfast 🍽️ 1 serving
~380
Calories
14g
Protein
12g
Fiber
55g
Carbs

Ingredients

  • ½ cup rolled oats (old-fashioned, not instant)
  • 1 cup water, milk, or almond milk
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • 1 cup mixed berries (fresh or frozen — don't thaw frozen)
  • 1 tbsp almond butter or peanut butter
  • 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup (optional)
  • ¼ tsp cinnamon (optional)
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. **Combine base:** In a small saucepan, add oats, liquid, chia seeds, salt, and cinnamon. Stir to mix.
  2. **Bring to boil:** Place saucepan over **medium-high heat** (level 6-7 of 10). Cook **3-4 minutes**, stirring occasionally with a wooden spoon, until mixture starts bubbling around the edges and small bubbles break the surface. If it boils over, reduce heat slightly and stir — the chia seeds can make it foam up.
  3. **Reduce heat:** Turn down to **medium-low** (level 3 of 10). You want a gentle simmer — small, slow bubbles, not a rolling boil. Simmer **3-5 minutes**, stirring every 30-45 seconds so it doesn't stick to the bottom. Oatmeal is done when:
  4. It's thick and creamy (coats the back of your spoon)
  5. Oats are tender when you bite one — no crunch in the center
  6. Liquid is mostly absorbed but it's still pourable
  7. If too thick (clumpy), stir in 2-3 tbsp more liquid
  8. If too thin (soupy), cook 1-2 more minutes
  9. **Transfer:** Remove from heat. Pour into a bowl immediately — it continues thickening as it sits.
  10. **Top:** Add berries while oatmeal is hot (this slightly warms fresh berries or helps frozen ones soften). Add almond butter in small dollops. Drizzle honey if desired.
  11. **Stir:** Mix everything together so the nut butter melts into swirls.

🧒 Kid Adaptation

  • Use all milk (creamier) instead of water
  • Let them add their own toppings (berries, sprinkles, chocolate chips)
  • Cut berries small for toddlers
  • Serve slightly cooled — hot oatmeal can burn
  • Add ½ sliced banana for extra kid appeal

📦 Batch Cooking

  • Make 4-5 servings at once. Store in airtight containers in fridge up to 5 days
  • Reheat: Add 2-3 tbsp milk, microwave 60-90 seconds, stir
  • Do NOT add berries until serving — they get mushy
  • Prep toppings in small containers for grab-and-go mornings
