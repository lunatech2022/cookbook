French Toast Sticks

⏱️ 15 min 📂 Breakfast 🍽️ 2 servings
~350
Calories
14g
Protein
4g
Fiber
45g
Carbs

🏷️ Tags

📦 Batch Friendly 🌅 Breakfast 🧒 Kid Friendly ⚡ Quick ⚡ Under 15 min 🌿 Vegetarian

Ingredients

  • 4 slices bread (thick-cut works best: brioche, Texas toast, or whole wheat)
  • 2 large eggs
  • ¼ cup milk (dairy or oat milk)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp butter (for pan)
  • Optional for serving: maple syrup, fresh berries, powdered sugar

Instructions

  1. **Cut bread:** Stack bread slices. Cut each slice into 3-4 strips (like long french fries). Set aside.
  2. **Make batter:** In a shallow bowl (big enough to fit a bread strip), whisk eggs, milk, vanilla, and cinnamon until fully combined and slightly frothy.
  3. **Heat pan:** Place a large non-stick skillet over **medium heat** (level 5 of 10). Add ½ tbsp butter and let it melt and foam, about 1 minute. Swirl to coat the pan. Test temperature: flick a tiny drop of water — it should sizzle gently. If it sizzles aggressively or butter browns instantly, reduce to medium-low. If it just sits there, increase to medium-high for 30 seconds then reduce. You want butter to foam steadily without browning.
  4. **Dip bread:** Working with 3-4 sticks at a time, dip each stick in the batter for **2-3 seconds per side**. Count it out: "one Mississippi, two Mississippi, three." Don't soak too long or bread gets soggy and falls apart. Lift and let excess drip off for 2 seconds before placing in pan.
  5. **Cook first side:** Place dipped sticks in the pan with at least ½ inch space between them — overcrowding drops pan temperature and causes steaming instead of browning. You should hear a gentle sizzle. Cook **2-3 minutes** without moving them. Peek under one edge at 2 minutes — you're looking for:
  6. **Golden brown:** Perfect, flip now
  7. **Pale blonde:** Give 30-60 more seconds
  8. **Dark brown:** Pan is too hot, reduce heat
  9. **Flip:** Turn each stick with tongs or spatula in one smooth motion. The cooked side should be evenly golden brown (not pale, not dark brown/black). Cook second side **2-3 minutes** until equally golden. The center should feel slightly firm when gently pressed — if it feels squishy/wet, give 30 more seconds.
  10. **Check doneness:** Cut one stick open. The inside should be cooked through (not wet or eggy), soft and custardy, not dry. If edges are brown but center is wet, reduce heat to medium-low and cook 1 more minute — the pan was too hot.
  11. **Batch cook:** Transfer cooked sticks to a plate. Wipe pan with paper towel if there are burnt butter bits. Add fresh ½ tbsp butter. Let it melt and foam. Adjust heat if needed. Repeat with remaining sticks.
  12. **Serve:** Stack sticks on plates. Serve warm with maple syrup for dipping, berries on the side, or a dusting of powdered sugar.

🧒 Kid Adaptation

  • Cut into smaller "fingers" for little hands
  • Serve with a small bowl of syrup for dipping (fun!)
  • Use cookie cutters for fun shapes instead of strips
  • Add sprinkles on top for color
  • Let them help with the dipping step

📦 Batch Cooking

  • Cook double batch, cool completely on a wire rack
  • Freeze in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to freezer bag
  • Reheat from frozen: Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 8-10 minutes
  • Microwave: 30-45 seconds (softer texture)
  • Store in freezer up to 2 months
