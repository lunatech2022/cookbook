Classic Guacamole

Ingredients

  • 3 ripe Hass avocados (see ripeness guide below)
  • ¼ cup finely diced white or yellow onion (about ½ small onion)
  • ¼ cup finely chopped fresh cilantro (packed)
  • 1 small jalapeño, seeded and finely minced (optional — see heat guide)
  • 1 large lime (for juice — about 2 tbsp)
  • ½ tsp salt (fine sea salt or kosher)
  • ¼ tsp ground cumin (optional but recommended)
  • 1 small Roma tomato, seeded and diced (optional — see note)
  • 1 small garlic clove, minced (optional)

Instructions

  1. **Dice onion:** Cut off both ends, peel, slice in half. Cut into thin slices, then crosswise into tiny ⅛-inch dice. The smaller the dice, the better the texture. Set aside.
  2. **Chop cilantro:** Bunch cilantro together. Chop finely, stems and all (stems have lots of flavor). You want about ¼ cup packed. Set aside.
  3. **Prep jalapeño (optional):** Cut in half lengthwise. Use a spoon to scrape out seeds and white membrane — that's where most heat lives. Mince finely. **Wash hands immediately after** — don't touch your eyes. Set aside.
  4. **Dice tomato (optional):** Cut Roma tomato in half lengthwise. Use your finger to scoop out seeds and watery pulp (prevents soggy guac). Dice flesh into ¼-inch pieces. Set aside.
  5. **Mince garlic (optional):** Peel clove. Chop extremely fine, or use a garlic press. Raw garlic is strong — a little goes a long way.
  6. **Juice lime:** Roll lime on counter under your palm for 10 seconds (releases more juice). Cut in half. Squeeze into a small bowl. Remove any seeds. You need about 2 tablespoons.
  7. **Halve avocados:** Hold avocado in one hand. Slice lengthwise around the pit, rotating as you go. Twist halves apart — one side will have the pit.
  8. **Remove pit:** Whack the pit firmly with the sharp edge of your knife blade so it sticks. Twist and pull out. **Never hold the avocado in your hand while stabbing** — use a cutting board.
  9. **Scoop flesh:** Use a large spoon to scoop avocado flesh into a medium bowl. If you see any brown spots, scrape them out with the edge of your spoon.
  10. **Mash:** Use a fork or potato masher to mash the avocado. Texture guide:
  11. **Chunky:** Mash lightly, leave visible pieces (good for dipping with sturdy chips)
  12. **Smooth:** Mash thoroughly until mostly uniform (good for spreading on toast)
  13. **Restaurant style:** Mash about 80% smooth, leave some small chunks for texture
  14. **Add onion, cilantro, jalapeño, garlic:** Add all prepped ingredients. Stir gently with a fork to combine. Don't over-stir or it gets pasty.
  15. **Add lime juice and salt:** Pour lime juice over the top. Sprinkle salt evenly. Add cumin if using. Fold in gently.
  16. **Taste test:** Dip a chip or clean spoon. Adjust:
  17. **Bland?** Add ⅛ tsp more salt, mix, taste again
  18. **Not tangy enough?** Add 1 tsp more lime juice
  19. **Too salty?** Add more mashed avocado or a tiny pinch of sugar
  20. **Too spicy?** Add more mashed avocado to dilute
  21. **Too mild?** Add a pinch more minced jalapeño or a dash of hot sauce
  22. **Fold in tomato (if using):** Add diced tomato last. Fold gently — don't mash the tomato pieces.
  23. **Final texture check:** Guacamole should be:
  24. Thick enough to hold its shape on a chip
  25. Not watery or runny
  26. Green and vibrant (not brown or gray)
  27. **Transfer to serving bowl:** Smooth the top with the back of a spoon.
  28. **Prevent browning (if not serving immediately):** Press plastic wrap directly onto the surface of the guacamole, eliminating all air contact. Or place avocado pits in the bowl (myth, but it helps slightly). Refrigerate up to 2 hours max.
  29. **Serve immediately** with tortilla chips, veggie sticks, or on toast. Guacamole is best within 30 minutes of making.

🧒 Kid Adaptation

  • Omit jalapeño completely
  • Serve with mild-flavored chips or veggie sticks (cucumber, bell pepper)
  • Let kids help mash the avocado with a fork (fun tactile activity)
  • Serve "build your own" style: plain mashed avocado in a bowl, toppings on the side
  • Add a tiny pinch of garlic powder instead of fresh garlic (milder flavor)

📦 Batch Cooking

  • **Best quality:** Within 30 minutes of making
  • **Acceptable:** Up to 2 hours, refrigerated with plastic wrap pressed to surface
  • **Not recommended:** Overnight — it WILL brown and lose flavor
  • Press plastic wrap directly on surface (no air pockets)
  • Add extra lime juice on top before covering (acid slows browning)
  • Some people add a thin layer of water on top, pour off before serving (works but dilutes slightly)
  • **For 2 people:** Use 2 avocados, halve other ingredients
  • **For a party (8+ people):** Use 6 avocados, double everything. Make in 2 batches rather than one huge bowl — easier to control texture.
