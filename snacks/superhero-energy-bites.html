Superhero" Energy Bites

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rolled oats (old-fashioned or quick)
  • ½ cup sunflower seed butter (or almond/peanut butter)
  • ⅓ cup pitted dates, chopped small (about 6 Medjool dates)
  • 2 tbsp honey or maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp ground flaxseed
  • 1 tbsp unsweetened cocoa powder
  • ½ tsp chicory root powder (optional — fiber boost)
  • ½ tsp cinnamon
  • 2 tbsp dried cranberries or raisins
  • 1 tsp orange zest (about ½ an orange)
  • 1 tbsp mini chocolate chips (optional)
  • 1 tbsp hemp seeds or chia seeds (optional)
  • 2 tbsp shredded coconut, or
  • 2 tbsp extra cocoa powder, or
  • 2 tbsp crushed seeds

Instructions

  1. **Chop dates:** If your dates are dry, soak them in warm water 5 minutes, then drain and chop fine. Sticky dates bind everything together.
  2. **Zest orange:** Grate the orange part of half an orange. Avoid the white pith — it's bitter.
  3. **Mix:** In a medium bowl, stir together oats, sunflower seed butter, chopped dates, honey, ground flaxseed, cocoa powder, chicory root powder, and cinnamon. Use a sturdy spoon or clean hands — it takes some effort.
  4. **Add mix-ins:** Fold in dried cranberries, orange zest, chocolate chips, and hemp seeds if using. Mix until evenly distributed.
  5. **Test consistency:** Pinch some mixture — it should hold together when squeezed. If too dry/crumbly, add 1 tsp water or milk and mix again. If too sticky, add 1 tbsp more oats.
  6. **Shape:** Scoop about 1 tbsp of mixture and roll into a ball between your palms. Repeat until you have about 14 bites.
  7. **Coat (optional):** Roll each bite in shredded coconut, cocoa powder, or crushed seeds for a fun outer layer.
  8. **Chill:** Place bites on a plate or small baking sheet. Refrigerate at least 20 minutes to firm up. They'll keep their shape much better when cold.
  9. **Store:** Transfer to an airtight container once firm.

🧒 Kid Adaptation

  • Call them "superhero power balls" — let them pick their "power" (speed, strength, etc.)
  • Kids can help roll the balls — slightly sticky hands are part of the fun
  • If they're sensitive to orange zest, reduce to ½ tsp or skip it
  • Roll in rainbow sprinkles instead of coconut for extra kid appeal
  • For younger kids, flatten into "cookies" instead of balls — easier to hold

📦 Batch Cooking

  • Keeps 1 week in the fridge in an airtight container
  • Freeze up to 2 months in a freezer bag — thaw 5 minutes before eating
  • Make a double batch on Sunday for the whole week
  • Pack 2-3 bites in small containers for school snacks
  • No need to reheat — eat straight from fridge or lunchbox
