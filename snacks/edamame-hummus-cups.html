Edamame Hummus Cups

⏱️ 5 min 📂 Snacks 🍽️ 2 servings
~280
Calories
14g
Protein
8g
Fiber
22g
Carbs

Ingredients

  • 1 cup shelled edamame (thawed if frozen)
  • 2 tbsp tahini or olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice (about ½ lemon)
  • 1 small garlic clove (or ¼ tsp garlic powder)
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 2-3 tbsp water (as needed)
  • 1 large cucumber (or 2 small)
  • ½ tsp everything bagel seasoning (optional)
  • Fresh dill or parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. **Thaw edamame:** If using frozen, place in a strainer and run under warm water for 1 minute. Drain well and pat dry with paper towels.
  2. **Prep garlic:** If using fresh garlic, peel the clove and roughly chop.
  3. **Blend:** In a food processor or blender, add edamame, tahini, lemon juice, garlic, and salt. Pulse 5-6 times to break down.
  4. **Add water:** With the motor running, drizzle in 2-3 tbsp water, one tablespoon at a time, until hummus is smooth and creamy but still thick enough to hold its shape. Scrape down sides as needed. Total blending time: 1-2 minutes.
  5. **Taste:** Add more salt, lemon, or garlic if needed. Transfer to a bowl.
  6. **Prep cucumber:** Wash cucumber. Cut off both ends. Slice into 1-inch thick rounds (you need 8 rounds).
  7. **Scoop seeds:** Use a small spoon or melon baller to gently scoop out the seeds from the center of each round, creating a "cup." Don't scoop all the way through — leave a bottom.
  8. **Fill:** Spoon 1-2 tbsp of edamame hummus into each cucumber cup. Press gently so it stays.
  9. **Top:** Sprinkle with everything bagel seasoning and fresh herbs.
  10. **Serve:** Best eaten within 30 minutes (cucumber releases water over time).
