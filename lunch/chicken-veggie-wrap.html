Chicken & Veggie Wrap

⏱️ 22 min 📂 Lunch 🍽️ 2 servings
~450
Calories
35g
Protein
8g
Fiber
35g
Carbs

🏷️ Tags

📦 Batch Friendly 🌾 High Fiber 🥩 High Protein 🥗 Lunch ⏱️ 16-30 min

Ingredients

  • 8 oz boneless skinless chicken breast (2 small breasts or 1 large halved)
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp olive oil or avocado oil
  • 2 whole wheat or high-fiber tortillas (8-inch)
  • ½ ripe avocado, sliced
  • 1 cup baby spinach or mixed greens
  • ½ cup shredded carrots
  • ½ cup sliced bell peppers (any color)
  • ¼ cup thinly sliced red onion (optional)
  • 2 tbsp hummus or Greek yogurt
  • Optional: 1 tsp hemp seeds or everything bagel seasoning

Instructions

  1. **Prep the chicken:** Remove from fridge 15 minutes before cooking. Pat completely dry with paper towels — wet chicken steams instead of sears. If one breast is thick (over 1 inch), slice it horizontally into two thinner pieces (called "butterflying") so it cooks evenly. Uneven thickness = dry edges and raw center.
  2. **Season:** Sprinkle both sides evenly with salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Press seasoning into the meat with your fingers so it adheres. Season right before cooking — salt draws out moisture if it sits too long.
  3. **Heat the pan:** Place a large skillet (cast iron is best, non-stick works) over **medium-high heat**. Let it heat 3-4 minutes. Test: flick a drop of water — it should sizzle and dance across the surface. If it evaporates instantly, reduce to medium. If it just sits there, keep heating.
  4. **Add oil:** Pour 1 tbsp oil into the pan. Swirl to coat. It should shimmer and move freely. If it starts smoking, remove from heat for 30 seconds.
  5. **Sear first side:** Lay chicken in the pan in a single layer. It should sizzle immediately and loudly — if it doesn't, pan isn't hot enough. **Do not move it.** Set timer:
  6. **Thin pieces (½ inch):** 4 minutes
  7. **Standard pieces (¾-1 inch):** 5-6 minutes
  8. **Thick pieces (1+ inches):** 6-7 minutes
  9. **Check for browning:** Peek under edge with spatula. It should be deep golden brown, not pale. If still pale, give it 1 more minute.
  10. **Flip:** Use tongs or spatula. Turn in one motion. The seared side should look caramelized (deep golden, not black). Cook second side:
  11. **Thin pieces:** 3-4 minutes
  12. **Standard pieces:** 4-5 minutes
  13. **Thick pieces:** 5-6 minutes
  14. **Check doneness (choose one):**
  15. **Thermometer (most reliable):** Insert into thickest part — must read **165°F (74°C)** for food safety
  16. **Visual check:** Cut into thickest part. Meat should be fully white/opaque, no pink. Juices should run clear, not pink or red
  17. **Touch test (practice):** Press with finger — firm with slight spring back = done. Soft and squishy = undercooked. Rock hard = overcooked
  18. **Rest:** Transfer to a cutting board. Let rest **5 minutes** — this is non-negotiable. Juices redistribute; cutting too early = dry chicken. Cover loosely with foil if needed.
  19. **Slice:** Cut against the grain (perpendicular to the muscle fibers) into thin strips. Against the grain = tender. With the grain = chewy.
  20. **Warm tortillas:** Heat each tortilla in a dry pan over medium heat for 20-30 seconds per side, or microwave 10 seconds wrapped in a damp paper towel. This prevents tearing.
  21. **Spread:** Spread 1 tbsp hummus or yogurt in the center of each tortilla, leaving a 1-inch border.
  22. **Layer:** Add spinach first, then chicken strips, carrots, bell peppers, avocado slices, and onion if using.
  23. **Season:** Sprinkle with hemp seeds or everything bagel seasoning.
  24. **Roll:** Fold the bottom edge up over the filling, then fold in the sides and roll tightly from bottom to top. Cut in half diagonally.

🧒 Kid Adaptation

  • Use a smaller 6-inch tortilla
  • Cut the wrap into 1-inch pinwheels (easier to hold)
  • Let kids choose which veggies go in
  • Add shredded cheese for familiarity
  • Serve deconstructed: chicken strips + veggies + dip on the side

📦 Batch Cooking

  • Grill 4-6 chicken breasts at once. Cool completely, slice, and refrigerate up to 4 days
  • Pre-chop veggies on Sunday and store in containers
  • Assemble wraps fresh each day — pre-made wraps get soggy
  • Chicken can be frozen for up to 3 months
