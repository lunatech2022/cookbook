Rainbow Barley Crunch Bowl

⏱️ 40 min 📂 Lunch 🍽️ 2 servings
~380
Calories
14g
Protein
14g
Fiber
52g
Carbs

🏷️ Tags

📦 Batch Friendly 🌾 High Fiber 🧒 Kid Friendly 🥗 Lunch 🍳 30+ min 🌿 Vegetarian

Ingredients

  • ½ cup pearled barley (dry), or 1½ cups cooked
  • 1½ cups water or low-sodium broth
  • Pinch of salt
  • ½ cup red cabbage, shredded fine
  • ½ cup carrot, shredded or cut into matchsticks
  • ½ cup cucumber, diced small
  • ¼ cup bell pepper (any color), diced small
  • ½ cup edamame, shelled (thawed if frozen)
  • 2 tbsp pumpkin seeds (pepitas)
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp fresh orange juice
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • ½ tsp orange zest (about ½ an orange)
  • ¼ tsp garlic powder (or 1 small clove, minced)
  • Pinch of salt and black pepper
  • ¼ cup chickpeas (extra legume hit)
  • 1 tbsp ground flaxseed (sprinkled on top)
  • Feta cheese crumbles (if not vegan)

Instructions

  1. **Rinse:** Put dry barley in a fine mesh strainer and rinse under cold water 30 seconds.
  2. **Boil:** In a small pot, combine barley, water/broth, and pinch of salt. Bring to a boil over high heat.
  3. **Simmer:** Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 20-25 minutes until barley is tender but still chewy. Pearled barley cooks faster than hulled.
  4. **Drain + cool:** If any liquid remains, drain it off. Spread barley on a plate to cool slightly while you prep toppings. Can use warm or cold.
  5. **Zest + juice:** Zest half an orange (fine grater — just the orange part). Juice that same orange half and the lemon half.
  6. **Whisk:** In a small jar or bowl, combine olive oil, orange juice, lemon juice, orange zest, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Shake or whisk until mixed.
  7. **Base:** Divide barley between two bowls.
  8. **Arrange colors:** Place each veggie in its own "section" around the bowl — red cabbage, orange carrot, green cucumber, yellow/orange bell pepper, green edamame. Kids eat with their eyes first.
  9. **Add crunch:** Sprinkle pumpkin seeds and parsley over the top.
  10. **Dress:** Drizzle half the dressing over each bowl. Serve the rest on the side.
  11. **Mix:** Let kids stir it all together themselves — they love the color explosion.

🧒 Kid Adaptation

  • Serve deconstructed: barley in one pile, veggies in small piles, dressing as a "dip"
  • Let them pick which colors to include (gives them control)
  • Dice everything smaller for toddlers — pea-sized is ideal
  • Swap edamame for sweet corn if they prefer it
  • Add a few raisins or dried cranberries for a sweet surprise
  • Call it a "rainbow treasure bowl" — they can "hunt" for each color

📦 Batch Cooking

  • Cook a big batch of barley on Sunday — keeps 5 days in the fridge
  • Pre-chop veggies and store in separate containers (they stay crisp)
  • Dressing keeps 5 days in a sealed jar in the fridge
  • Assemble fresh each day — dressed barley gets soggy after 24 hours
  • Pre-portion into small containers for grab-and-go lunches
