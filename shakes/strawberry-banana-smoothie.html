Strawberry Banana Smoothie

⏱️ 5 min 📂 Shakes 🍽️ 1 serving
~280
Calories
16g
Protein
6g
Fiber
48g
Carbs

Ingredients

  • 1 cup frozen strawberries (do not thaw)
  • 1 ripe banana (fresh or frozen — frozen makes it thicker)
  • ½ cup plain Greek yogurt
  • ½ cup milk (dairy, almond, or oat)
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds or ground flaxseed
  • 1 tsp honey or maple syrup (optional — banana adds sweetness)
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • 3-4 ice cubes (if using fresh banana)

Instructions

  1. **Prep banana:** Peel banana. Break into 3-4 chunks. If using fresh banana, add 3-4 ice cubes for thickness.
  2. **Layer blender:** Add liquid first (milk), then yogurt, then banana chunks, then frozen strawberries on top. This helps the blender pull ingredients down. Add chia seeds, honey, and vanilla.
  3. **Blend low first:** Start blender on LOW speed for 10-15 seconds to break up large pieces.
  4. **Blend high:** Increase to HIGH speed. Blend 30-45 seconds until completely smooth. Stop and scrape down sides if needed. No chunks should remain.
  5. **Check consistency:** If too thick to pour, add 2-3 tbsp more milk and blend 10 more seconds. If too thin, add ½ frozen banana or 2 more ice cubes.
  6. **Serve immediately:** Pour into a glass. Drink within 10 minutes — separation starts quickly.

🧒 Kid Adaptation

  • Use vanilla yogurt instead of plain (sweeter)
  • Skip the honey — banana is sweet enough for kids
  • Serve in a fun cup with a straw
  • Make it a "milkshake" by adding 1 tbsp cocoa powder
  • Let them press the blender button (supervised)

📦 Batch Cooking

  • Pre-make smoothie packs: Portion frozen fruit into freezer bags (1 cup strawberries + 1 banana per bag)
  • Freeze up to 3 months
  • Morning of: Dump pack in blender, add yogurt, milk, blend
  • Can also freeze blended smoothie into popsicle molds
  • Do NOT make ahead and refrigerate — separation and texture loss
