Orange Creamsicle Power Smoothie

Ingredients

  • 1 medium frozen banana (break into chunks before freezing)
  • ½ cup plain Greek yogurt
  • ½ cup orange juice (fresh-squeezed or no-pulp)
  • ¼ cup rolled oats
  • 2 tbsp milk (dairy or oat)
  • 1 tbsp ground flaxseed
  • ½ tsp chicory root powder
  • ½ tsp orange zest (about ½ an orange)
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • ¼ tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp honey or maple syrup (optional — orange juice is usually sweet enough)
  • ½ cup ice cubes (less if banana was frozen solid)
  • 1 tbsp hemp seeds
  • Small orange slice for garnish

Instructions

  1. **Prep banana:** Peel ripe bananas, break into chunks, and freeze at least 4 hours (or overnight). Frozen banana makes it thick and creamy without ice cream.
  2. **Zest orange:** Grate the orange part only — avoid the white pith. You need about ½ tsp.
  3. **Load blender:** Add frozen banana, Greek yogurt, orange juice, rolled oats, milk, ground flaxseed, chicory root powder, orange zest, vanilla, and cinnamon. Add honey if you want it sweeter.
  4. **Blend:** Start on low speed 10 seconds to break up banana, then increase to high. Blend 30-45 seconds until completely smooth and thick.
  5. **Add ice:** Drop in ice cubes. Blend another 15-20 seconds. If too thick to blend, add 1 tbsp more milk. If too thin, add 2-3 more ice cubes or another frozen banana chunk.
  6. **Taste:** It should be sweet, creamy, and bright from the orange zest. Add a touch more honey if needed (rarely necessary).
  7. **Serve:** Pour into a tall glass. Sprinkle hemp seeds on top. Garnish with an orange slice if you're feeling fancy.

🧒 Kid Adaptation

  • Call it an "orange milkshake" — they'll never know it's healthy
  • Let them help zest the orange and press the blender buttons
  • Use a fun straw (reusable silicone with a shape)
  • For younger kids, serve in a sippy cup — it's thick enough not to spill easily
  • Skip chicory root the first time if they're picky; add it once they love the base recipe

📦 Batch Cooking

  • Pre-portion frozen banana chunks into small freezer bags (1 banana per bag)
  • Mix dry ingredients (oats, flax, chicory, cinnamon) in small containers — just add to blender with wet ingredients
  • Smoothie is best fresh, but keeps in fridge up to 24 hours in a sealed jar (shake well before drinking)
  • Do NOT freeze the finished smoothie — texture gets weird
